Video as motion topology

A compact graph of what moved, where, when, and why.

Core Idea

Instead of carrying video as a giant stack of pixel frames, Axiom can treat video like a topology: a structured graph of which objects exist, how they move, how they interact, and what those movements mean. The map is not literal geometry — it is a compressed representation built from entity identity, motion vectors, keyframe boundaries, spatial adjacency, causal chains, and inferred events.

Where Text AI learns symbol patterns and Audio AI learns wave/groove patterns, Video AI can learn spatial-time patterns. The model stops carrying the whole reel and starts carrying the storyboard.

Micro-Agent Approach

The topology map can be split into smaller specialist agents. Each agent inspects one type of structure, emits a compact signed report, and hands it to a Coordinator. Modular instead of monolithic — and the Coordinator activates only the agents the task needs.

AgentListens forExample output
ObjectAgent Entity detection + persistence hand_01, cup_01, liquid_01 visible across frames 0–61
MotionAgent Trajectory direction, velocity, acceleration hand_01 reaches toward cup_01 at 1.2 m/s
KeyframeAgent Scene cuts, event seams, action boundaries major boundary at frame 24 (grip event)
ContainmentAgent Spatial relationships between entities hand_01 in contact with cup_01 from frame 24–39
CausalChainAgent Temporal cause → effect inference cup tilt  ⇒  liquid exit (high confidence)
EmotionAgent Face + body affect signals actor expression: focused, no distress
IntentAgent 6-class action classification (shared with audio + text) INFORM — instrumental action, not commanded

Activation savings

The key efficiency move is the same as the audio engine: selective activation. A surveillance task may only need MotionAgent + KeyframeAgent. A toy-camera safety check may only need EmotionAgent + IntentAgent. A surgery review may need ObjectAgent + ContainmentAgent + CausalChainAgent. The Coordinator routes accordingly.

Lower compute, lower data movement, and an explicit audit trail because each active agent produces a small evidence report.

Pipeline: preserve causal meaning while reducing raw data movement, token use, and unexplained decisions.

Use cases unlocked

  • AI toys with cameras — verify what a child did near a toy without storing raw video. "The toy detected: reach → grip → cup tilt → call parent."
  • Smart-home cameras — replace "AI says it saw a person" with a signed event chain a regulator can replay.
  • Dashcam + driving safety — verified incident reports for insurance; the chain is signed at the edge.
  • Sports analytics — automatic action tagging without manual frame review.
  • Content moderation at scale — TikTok / Instagram detect a fight scene from the motion topology, not from raw frame inspection.
  • Sign-language translation — temporal chains of hand-shape transitions feed directly into the text intent classifier.
  • Surgery review — every move in a procedure is a signed evidence chain.
  • Robotic vision — robots emit their own video graph blocks so a human can audit "what the robot thinks it saw."

Positioning statement

Axiom Temporal Surface Map is a proposed sensory-governance layer that converts video into measurable motion topology, routes the topology to specialist micro-agents, and produces compact evidence-backed event chains for reasoning, verification, and energy-aware AI workflows.

Status

This is a concept, not a validated benchmark result. The next step would be a small prototype that:

  1. Extracts keyframes + object tracks from a short clip using an off-the-shelf detector (YOLO, MediaPipe);
  2. Runs 2–3 of the simplest agents (Object, Motion, Keyframe);
  3. Compares event-detection accuracy + token / compute cost against passing the raw video to a multi-modal LLM.

Pass criteria mirror the audio plan: ≥80% agreement with human label on a 30-clip corpus; ≤1 KB block per 4-second clip; p99 latency ≤ 200 ms per second of video on a CPU-only box.
